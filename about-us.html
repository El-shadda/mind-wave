Learn About Our Mission

Transforming Mental Health at KUHeS

MindWave is a unified mental health initiative designed to empower KUHeS students with knowledge, resilience, and healthy mental health-seeking habits. Our program brings students together under one vision: to normalize conversations about mental well-being and promote a culture of support.

Founded in response to alarming mental health statistics among college students, MindWave was established by the KUHeS Student Representative Council (SRC) Welfare Directors in collaboration with Harmony Mental Wellness Solutions Clinic, founded by Prof. Chiwoza Bandawe.

The initiative began at the Blantyre campus and has expanded to include Lilongwe campus, creating a unified voice for mental health advocacy across all KUHeS campuses.

Our Mission & Vision

The guiding principles that drive our work at MindWave

Our Mission

To empower a student community that acknowledges the relevance of mental health issues and to build a culture where seeking help is seen as strength, not weakness. We aim to equip students with the confidence to understand mental health topics, seek support when needed, and develop healthy coping habits in a world that often judges rather than understands.

Our Vision

To craft minds and nourish a safe space of students ready to navigate the beautiful maze of life. We envision a KUHeS community where mental health conversations are normalized, support systems are readily accessible, and every student feels empowered to prioritize their mental well-being.

Our Objectives

The specific goals we're working to achieve through the MindWave initiative

Sustainable Intervention

Develop a sustainable mental health intervention program at KUHeS that continues to support students year after year.

Professional Connections

Connect students with mental health professionals for accessible support anytime, especially in urgent situations.

Inclusive Environment

Create a warm and inclusive environment through KUHeS Chat Point, our flagship peer support platform.

Institutionalization

Institutionalize the initiative, ensuring each year's SRC Welfare Directors pass it down to their successors.

Assessment & Screening

To assess and screen common mental health disorders among students for early detection and intervention.

Coping Mechanisms

Equip students with strong decision-making skills and effective mental health coping mechanisms.

Executive Committee

The central governing body of MindWave consisting of:

  • President
  • Vice President
  • Engagement Officer
  • Club Administrator
  • Events Coordinator
  • Patron

Key Responsibilities

The Executive Committee is responsible for:

  • Coordinating all activities of MindWave
  • Proposing and approving budgets
  • Supervising elections and appointments
  • Establishing policies and procedures
  • Making legislation available to all members

Membership

MindWave membership is:

  • Free to all registered KUHeS students
  • Active until graduation or withdrawal
  • Open to participation in all activities
  • Entitled to all services and benefits
  • Subject to the rights outlined in our constitution
